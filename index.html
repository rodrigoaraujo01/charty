Create charts directly from Shortcuts

Charty gives you 22 powerful new actions for Shortcuts,
allowing you to plot data directly from it!

Charty gives you 22 powerful new actions for Shortcuts, allowing you to plot data directly from it!

5 Series types

Use bar, line, scatter, pie, and donut charts to visualize your data in any way you'd like. Or combine them, to create richer charts.

5 Widget options

On iOS 14, you can now add any of your charts as a widget on your homescreen.

22 Powerful actions

Charty adds powerful actions to Apple's Shortcuts app, enabling users to create, export charts and much more.

42 Examples included

Including visualizations of math functions, health data, custom data, and much more.
Complete with chart previews and descriptions to get you started.

Charty examples

Each of the examples displayed below are available in the Example's gallery inside the app.

Sine & Cosine

Uses one repeat and some calculations actions to compute the values of sine and cosine between -π and π.

Circle with Noise

Uses one repeat actions and lots of calculations to calculate the points that create a circle with radius 1 using the x² + y² = 1 equation. It also calculates 84 other random points around the circle to add some noise behind it.

Heart Rate Analysis

Uses two Find Health Sample actions, to get daily Resting Heart Rate and Heart Rate Variability measurements from the last 30 days.

Compare Distances

Uses Find Health Sample action to extract daily walked+ran distances for the last 45 days, the calculates average values for the first 30 days, the last 15 and all values.

Donut from CSV

Uses the Add Series From CSV action to create a donut chart from a csv file.

Stacked Monthly Expenses

Processes expenses stored on a text action to create monthly stacked bars representing expenditure on 5 categories.
